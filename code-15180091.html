1. Full Legal Description

CN 1518 00 91 — Heading 1518 (Chapter 15)

“Animal, vegetable or microbial fats and oils and their fractions, boiled, oxidised, dehydrated, sulphurised, blown, polymerised by heat in vacuum or in inert gas or otherwise chemically modified (excl. those of heading 1516 and linoxyn)”

Qualifying modification methods (the oil must have undergone at least one):

  • Boiling — heating to boiling point causing polymerisation
  • Oxidation — reaction with oxygen (e.g., drying oils)
  • Dehydration — removal of water with chemical restructuring
  • Sulphurisation — reaction with sulphur (e.g., factice)
  • Blowing — air oxidation at elevated temperature
  • Polymerisation — heat-induced polymerisation under vacuum/inert gas
  • Otherwise chemically modified — epoxidation, maleinisation, etc.

Simple refining (degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, deodorisation) does NOT constitute chemical modification for this heading. Refined oils stay under their original heading.

3. Countervailing Duty Detail (Reg. EU 2019/2092)

CVD on biodiesel from Indonesia
Scope: ex 1518 00 91, ex 1518 00 95, ex 1518 00 99

The CVD targets biodiesel products — defined as fatty acid mono-alkyl esters (FAME) or hydrotreated vegetable oils (HVO) used as fuel. The scope explicitly includes ex 1518 00 91 when the chemically modified oil is used as biodiesel.

Crude feedstock vs finished biodiesel
The CVD targets the finished biodiesel product, not the crude feedstock. If a chemically modified oil is imported as feedstock for further processing into biodiesel at an EU facility, it may fall outside the CVD scope. However, this is a narrow distinction that customs will scrutinise closely.

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Chemically modified oils
  • Epoxidised soybean oil (ESBO — plasticiser)
  • Epoxidised linseed oil
  • Blown rapeseed oil (lubricant base)
  • Polymerised linseed oil (varnish)
  • Maleinised oils (chemical intermediate)
  • Sulphurised oils (factice, extreme-pressure additives)
Industrial applications
  • PVC plasticisers (epoxidised oils)
  • Paints, varnishes, coatings (drying oils)
  • Lubricants and metalworking fluids
  • Rubber processing (factice)
  • Adhesives and sealants
  • Biodiesel (if transesterified to FAME)

8. Relevance to Rubber Seed Oil

Assessment: LOW relevance — conditional
Criterion1518 00 91Rubber seed oil
Chemical stateChemically modified (epoxidised, polymerised, etc.)Unmodified crude triglyceride oil
ClassificationChapter 15 (modified oils)Chapter 15 (unmodified oils, heading 1515)
Applicability to RSOOnly if RSO undergoes chemical modification before importCrude RSO stays under 1515 90

Bottom line: Crude rubber seed oil has not been chemically modified and does not qualify for this code. If the exporter epoxidised the RSO before import (creating epoxidised rubber seed oil for plasticiser use), it could fall under 1518 00 91 at 0% MFN — but this is a different product with a different market and cannot be used as a shortcut for crude RSO imports.
