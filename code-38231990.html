CN 3823 19 90 — Acid Oils from Refining

MFN: 2.9% ADD for Indonesia: 46.4% Effective: 49.3% Chapter 38 — Section 6 GSP non-sensitive

Industrial monocarboxylic fatty acids and acid oils from refining — the acidulated by-product of alkali refining of crude oils (soapstock treated with sulphuric acid). Despite a low MFN rate of 2.9%, this code carries a 46.4% anti-dumping duty for Indonesia (Reg. 2023/111), making it the most expensive classification path for Indonesian-origin fatty products.

1. Full Legal Description

CN 3823 19 90 — Heading 3823 (Chapter 38)

“Industrial monocarboxylic fatty acids; acid oils from refining” — the catch-all subheading for industrial fatty acids and acid oils that are not classified as stearic acid (3823 11), oleic acid (3823 12), tall oil fatty acids (3823 13), distilled fatty acids (3823 19 10), or fatty acid distillate (3823 19 30).

What this product actually is: The acidulated soapstock from alkali refining of crude oils. When crude oil is treated with caustic soda (NaOH) to remove free fatty acids, the resulting soapstock is then acidulated with sulphuric acid to release the fatty acids. This product is characterised by:

  • High FFA: typically 50–90% free fatty acids
  • Dark colour: brown to black, due to impurities and oxidation
  • High unsaponifiable matter: often 2–8%
  • Mixed glyceride/FFA composition: partial glycerides coexist with free fatty acids

Critical: GSP does NOT eliminate the ADD
Even if Indonesia's Section 6 GSP preferences reduce the MFN duty to 0%, the anti-dumping duty of 46.4% still applies. ADD is levied independently of MFN/preferential rates. The effective duty for Indonesian-origin products under this code is always at least 46.4% (if GSP applies) or 49.3% (at full MFN + ADD).

Reg. (EU) 2023/111 — Full text

Biofuel certification exclusion

RED II exemption
Fatty acids certified for sustainable biofuels production under Directive (EU) 2018/2001 (Renewable Energy Directive II) are excluded from the ADD scope. To qualify, the importer must present evidence that the product will be used for biofuel production meeting RED II sustainability criteria. This requires specific certification (e.g., ISCC EU certification) and documentation at import.

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Refining by-products
  • Acidulated soapstock from soybean oil refining
  • Acid oil from palm oil alkali refining
  • Acid oils from sunflower oil refining
  • Acidulated refining residue from rapeseed/canola oil
  • Mixed acid oils from multi-feedstock refineries
  • Coconut acid oils (excl. distilled — those are 3823 19 10)
Industrial applications
  • Biodiesel feedstock (after esterification)
  • Animal feed (after treatment, under TARIC ...30)
  • Soap and detergent manufacturing
  • Fatty acid distillation feedstock
  • Industrial chemical intermediate
  • Metalworking fluids and lubricants

8. Relevance to Rubber Seed Oil

Assessment: NOT a viable classification for RSO
RSO cannot be classified under 3823 19 90

Despite rubber seed oil having a similar chemical profile (high FFA 25–35%) to acid oils from refining, it is fundamentally a different product for classification purposes:

Criterion Acid oil (3823 19 90) Rubber seed oil
OriginBy-product of alkali refiningPrimary product of seed extraction
ProcessSoapstock → acidulationPressing/solvent extraction
Glyceride structureMostly free fatty acids (50–90%)Mixed triglycerides + FFA (25–35%)
DoS (degree of split)Variable (can be ≥ or < 97%)Low (not split) — mostly intact triglycerides
EU classificationChapter 38 (industrial chemical)Chapter 15 (fixed vegetable oil)

Bottom line: Classifying RSO under 3823 19 90 would be a clear misclassification (wrong heading) that additionally triggers a 49.3% effective duty. The correct classification for RSO as industrial feedstock is 1515 90 40 at 3.2%.
