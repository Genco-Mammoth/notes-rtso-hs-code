Non-Tariff Measures Applicable to HS 1515.90

Primary sources: EUR-Lex regulations TARIC codes listed

Beyond customs duty rates, several non-tariff measures may apply to imports of HS 1515.90 products. These include CITES controls, organic product certification, waste shipment controls, and autonomous duty suspensions for specific end uses.

CITES Controls (Reg. (EU) 2023/966)

When does CITES apply to vegetable oils?

CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora) applies only if the plant species from which the oil is derived is listed in the Annexes to Regulation (EC) No 338/97. For most common vegetable oils under heading 1515 90 (shea, mango, hazelnut, avocado, etc.), CITES does not apply.

However, certain rare or wild-harvested plant species may trigger CITES requirements. Importers should verify whether their specific botanical source species is CITES-listed.

Reg. (EU) 2023/966

Organic Products Inspection (Reg. (EU) 2021/2306)

When does the organic requirement apply?

If the goods bear an organic production reference in their labelling, advertising, or accompanying documents, a Certificate of Inspection (COI) must be submitted at import. This is document code C644 in the TARIC system.

The requirement implements Regulation (EU) 2018/848 on organic production and labelling. It applies to all third countries except Switzerland, Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway (which are treated as equivalent under the EEA).

For Indonesia: Indonesian organic oil producers must obtain the COI through the EU’s TRACES system before export. Non-organic oils are not affected by this measure.

Reg. (EU) 2021/2306

Waste Shipment Control (Reg. (EC) 1013/2006)

Relevance to heading 1515.90

This measure is relevant primarily when the oils being imported are classified as waste or by-products (e.g., spent oils, production residues). For standard commercial vegetable oil imports intended for food, cosmetic, or pharmaceutical use, this measure typically does not apply.

If applicable, prior written notification and consent are required for Annex IV (amber-list) waste shipments. Green-list wastes with hazardous characteristics are treated as amber-listed.

Reg. (EC) 1013/2006

Autonomous Duty Suspension: Ships & Platforms (EU003)

Duty-free import for ship/platform incorporation

Under the Special Provisions of Section II(A)(3) of the Preliminary Provisions of the Combined Nomenclature, customs duties are suspended for goods intended for incorporation into:

  • Specified categories of ships, boats, and other vessels
  • Drilling or production platforms

This requires an end-use authorisation (certificate code C990) from the importing Member State’s customs authority. The oils must genuinely be intended for use in ship or platform construction, repair, maintenance, conversion, or equipping.

Practical relevance
This suspension is rarely relevant for vegetable oil imports, as these oils are typically imported for food, cosmetic, or pharmaceutical use — not for ship construction. It may apply in niche cases (e.g., oils used as lubricants or biodegradable hydraulic fluids in marine applications).

Sanctions: Import Bans on Specific Territories

Import prohibitions

Two EU sanctions regulations impose import bans on goods from specific territories. While these do not directly affect Indonesian imports, they are listed in TARIC for all codes under 1515 90:

Crimea / Sevastopol (Reg. 692/2014)

Prohibition on import of goods originating in Crimea or Sevastopol, with exceptions for goods examined and certified by Ukrainian authorities under the EU-Ukraine Association Agreement.

Non-government controlled areas of Ukraine (Reg. 2022/263)

Prohibition on import of goods originating in non-government controlled areas of Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk, and Zaporizhzhia oblasts. Exceptions for pre-23 February 2022 trade contracts and EU-UA Association Agreement certified goods.
