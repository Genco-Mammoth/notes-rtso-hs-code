CN 1515 90 59 — Crude Oils, Solid >1 kg or Fluid

MFN: 6.4% RU/BY: 50% Indonesia: 6.4% No GSP for Indonesia

This code covers crude (unrefined) fixed vegetable or microbial fats and oils for food, cosmetic, or pharmaceutical use (non-technical), in bulk packaging (solid in packings > 1 kg, or in liquid/fluid form). It is one of the two codes in focus for this report.

Full Legal Description

CN 1515 90 59 00

“Other fixed vegetable fats and oils and their fractions, whether or not refined, but not chemically modified — Other oils and their fractions — Crude oils — Solid, other; fluid”

Excluding: oils for technical or industrial uses (those are 1515 90 40); and the specifically named oils (soya-bean, peanut, olive, palm, sunflower, safflower, cotton-seed, coconut, palm kernel, babassu, rubsen, mustard seed, linseed, maize germ, castor, tung, sesame, jojoba or oiticica oil; myrtle wax, japan wax, tobacco seed oil and microbial oils).

Bottom line for Indonesia
Indonesia pays the full MFN rate of 6.4%. Section 3 (Animal and vegetable oils) was graduated from the EU GSP scheme on 1 January 2020. The EU-Indonesia CEPA, though concluded in September 2025, has not yet entered into force. No preferential arrangement currently reduces this rate.

How the GSP Sensitive Rate Would Work (If Available)

For Standard GSP beneficiaries where Section 3 has not been graduated, the GSP “sensitive product” reduction applies. Under Article 7 of Regulation 978/2012, this is a 3.5 percentage-point reduction on the MFN ad valorem rate:

Calculation (hypothetical — if Indonesia still had GSP)
MFN rate 6.4%
GSP sensitive reduction (3.5 pp) −3.5 pp
GSP preferential rate 2.9%

This is why some databases list a 2.9% GSP rate for this code. That rate would apply to Standard GSP beneficiaries without a Section 3 graduation. Since Indonesia’s Section 3 was graduated, it does not apply.
