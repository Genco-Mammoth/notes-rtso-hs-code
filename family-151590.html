Pattern Analysis: What Drives the Rate Differences?

Factor 1: Crude vs. Refined (3.2% → 5.1%)

Comparing the technical/industrial codes: crude oils (1515 90 40) carry 3.2%, while refined ones (1515 90 60) carry 5.1%. The EU generally applies lower duties on crude/raw materials (which European industry may further process) and higher duties on finished/processed goods to protect EU value-added producers.

Factor 2: End Use (3.2% → 6.4%)

Comparing crude oils: technical/industrial use (1515 90 40) carries 3.2%, while food/cosmetic use (1515 90 59) carries 6.4%. This doubles the rate. Food-grade oils compete with EU-produced edible oils, warranting higher protection than industrial inputs.

Factor 3: Packaging Size (6.4% → 12.8%)

Comparing crude food-grade oils: bulk/fluid (1515 90 59) carries 6.4%, while small packings ≤1 kg (1515 90 51) carries 12.8%. Small retail packings indicate consumer-ready products that compete directly with EU retail brands, justifying the highest rate in the family.

Factor 4: All Combined

The lowest rate (3.2%) applies to crude industrial inputs — the least competitive threat. The highest rate (12.8%) applies to small-packaged, consumer-ready, food/cosmetic oils — the most direct competitive threat to EU producers. Our two focus codes (6.4% and 9.6%) sit squarely in the middle.
