CN 3823 19 10 — Distilled Fatty Acids

MFN: 2.9% ADD: 46.4% (DoS ≥97%) Effective: 49.3% Chapter 38 — Section 6

Purified fatty acids obtained by fractional distillation of crude fatty acid mixtures. These are higher-purity products than acid oils, and by definition typically have a degree of split ≥97% — placing them squarely within the anti-dumping duty scope for Indonesia.

1. Full Legal Description

CN 3823 19 10 — Heading 3823 (Chapter 38)

“Industrial monocarboxylic fatty acids; acid oils from refining — Distilled fatty acids” — high-purity fatty acids separated by fractional distillation of crude fatty acid mixtures. The distillation process splits fatty acids by chain length or removes impurities, producing a product with a high degree of split (typically ≥95%).

Excludes: stearic acid (3823 11), oleic acid (3823 12), tall oil fatty acids (3823 13), and fatty acid distillate (3823 19 30).

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Distilled products
  • Fractionally distilled coconut fatty acids
  • Distilled palm kernel fatty acids
  • Purified mixed vegetable fatty acids (C8–C18 cuts)
  • Distilled tallow fatty acids
  • High-purity medium-chain fatty acid fractions
Industrial applications
  • Oleochemical intermediates (esters, alcohols, amides)
  • Cosmetic and personal care ingredients
  • Metalworking fluid formulations
  • Plasticiser and lubricant additives
  • Rubber processing chemicals

8. Relevance to Rubber Seed Oil

Assessment: NOT applicable

Rubber seed oil is a crude glyceride oil from seed extraction — it has not undergone fractional distillation. Even if RSO were hydrolysed and distilled (which would be a different industrial process entirely), the resulting distilled rubber seed fatty acids would be a completely different product from crude RSO. The correct path for crude RSO is 1515 90 40 at 3.2%.
