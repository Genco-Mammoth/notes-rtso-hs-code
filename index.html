The Short Answer
For crude rubber seed oil from Indonesia as biodiesel feedstock: CN 1515 90 40 at 3.2% duty

This is the single correct, defensible, and lowest-duty classification available. Classifying as “crude fixed vegetable oil for technical/industrial use” saves 50% versus the default 1515 90 59 (6.4%). No anti-dumping or countervailing duties apply. All alternatives (0% codes like 1522 00 99, 1518 00 31) are either indefensible or carry significant trade-defence risks. Read the full analysis on the Rubber Seed Oil Strategy page.

At a Glance

The recommended code is 1515 90 40 at 3.2%

For crude rubber seed oil as biodiesel feedstock, this is the correct, defensible, and lowest-duty classification. It requires documenting that the oil is crude (mechanically extracted, not refined) and for industrial use (biodiesel production, not food).

All 0% codes are traps or conditional

Codes with 0% duty (1522 00 99, 1518 00 31, 1518 00 91) look attractive but are either indefensible (RSO is not a residue, not chemically modified) or conditional (must be a genuine blend of 2+ oils). Chapter 38 codes carry 46.4% ADD for Indonesia.

BTI is essential before first shipment

Obtain a Binding Tariff Information ruling for 1515 90 40 from the importing Member State. Valid for 3 years, binding across all 27 EU countries. Also prepare an evidence pack: end-use declaration, supply agreement, certificate of analysis.

CEPA will change everything — eventually

The EU-Indonesia CEPA (concluded September 2025, not yet in force) will progressively eliminate tariffs. Once ratified, RSO duties could drop to 0%. Until then, Indonesia pays full MFN and faces a 6–10 pp cost gap vs. FTA competitors.
