Glossary of Terms

60+ terms Alphabetical Cross-referenced

Definitions of all technical terms, abbreviations, legal instruments, and classification concepts used throughout this report. Organised alphabetically for quick lookup.

A

Acid oil
The acidulated by-product of alkali refining of crude oils. Soapstock is treated with sulphuric acid to release free fatty acids. Classified under 3823 19 90. Not the same as a crude oil with naturally high FFA.
Acid value
A measure of free fatty acid content, expressed in mg KOH per gram of oil. Higher acid value = higher FFA. Crude rubber seed oil typically has an acid value of 50–70 mg KOH/g.
ADD (Anti-Dumping Duty)
A duty imposed on imports that are priced below their normal value (i.e., below the domestic price in the exporting country). Counters unfair pricing. For Indonesian fatty acids: Reg. (EU) 2023/111, rates 15.2%–46.4%.
Ad valorem
A duty calculated as a percentage of the customs value (CIF). Most EU duties on vegetable oils are ad valorem. Contrast with specific duties (per kg).
Anti-circumvention
Measures to prevent exporters from evading ADD/CVD by routing goods through third countries or modifying products slightly.

B–C

BTI (Binding Tariff Information)
A legally binding ruling from an EU Member State customs authority on the tariff classification of a specific product. Valid for 3 years, binding across all 27 EU Member States. Applied for via the EBTI system.
CET (Common External Tariff)
Same as CCT (Common Customs Tariff). The uniform tariff applied by all EU Member States to goods imported from non-EU countries.
CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)
The customs valuation basis: the price paid for the goods plus insurance and freight to the EU border. EU ad valorem duties are calculated on the CIF value.
CJEU (Court of Justice of the European Union)
The EU's supreme court. Its rulings on tariff classification (e.g., Case C-233/15) are binding precedent for all EU customs authorities.
CN (Combined Nomenclature)
The EU's 8-digit customs coding system. Extends the 6-digit HS code with 2 EU-specific digits (7–8). Updated annually. Determines the duty rate.
Crude oil
An oil that has only been extracted (mechanically pressed or solvent extracted) and filtered. No degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, or deodorisation. High FFA alone does not make an oil “refined.”
CVD (Countervailing Duty)
A duty imposed on imports that benefit from government subsidies in the exporting country. For Indonesian biodiesel: Reg. (EU) 2019/2092, rates 8%–18%.

D–F

Degras
A residue from the treatment of wool or animal hair. Classified under 1522 00 10 at 3.8% MFN.
Degumming
A refining step that removes phospholipids (gums) from crude oil using water or acid. An oil that has been degummed is classified as “refined” for CN purposes.
Deodorisation
A refining step using steam under high vacuum to remove volatile compounds (odour, flavour). Changes classification from crude to refined.
DoS (Degree of Split)
The percentage of fatty acid bonds hydrolysed from glycerol. DoS ≥97% = nearly pure free fatty acids. The EU ADD regulation uses DoS ≥97% as its scope threshold for Indonesian fatty acids.
Dumping
Selling goods in an export market at a price below the normal value (usually the domestic price in the exporting country). The basis for anti-dumping duties.
Dutch clause
Not a formal term, but refers to the practice in EU customs of applying the strictest classification rule across all Member States for consistency.
EBA (Everything But Arms)
The most generous GSP arrangement, providing duty-free, quota-free access for all products (except arms) from UN-designated Least Developed Countries.
EBTI (European Binding Tariff Information)
The centralised EU database of all BTI decisions. Searchable at ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/dds2/bti.
End use
The purpose for which goods are imported. For heading 1515 90, the distinction between technical/industrial use (code 40/60) and other use (code 51/59/91/99) is based on end use — one of the few CN codes where end use directly determines the subheading.
ERGA OMNES
Latin for “towards all.” The standard MFN duty rate applied to all countries without preferential agreements. Same as MFN.
Essential character (GIR 3(b))
The factor that gives a mixture its principal identity. For oil blends, usually determined by the dominant component by weight/volume.
EUDR (EU Deforestation Regulation)
Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, requiring importers to prove certain commodities (including palm oil, soy, cattle, wood) are deforestation-free. May apply to some vegetable oil supply chains.
FFA (Free Fatty Acids)
Fatty acids that have been hydrolysed from their glycerol backbone. High FFA (25–35%) in crude rubber seed oil is normal and does not change classification. FFA is measured as acid value.
Fixed oil
A non-volatile vegetable or microbial fat/oil (as opposed to essential/volatile oils). All oils under Chapter 15 are fixed oils.

G–I

GIR (General Interpretative Rules)
The six rules that govern classification of goods under the Harmonized System. Applied in sequence: GIR 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6. For mixtures, GIR 2(b) and 3(a)–(c) are critical.
GSP (Generalised Scheme of Preferences)
The EU's unilateral preferential tariff system for developing countries. Three tiers: Standard GSP (reduced duties), GSP+ (duty-free, conditioned on conventions), EBA (duty-free for LDCs). Established by Reg. (EU) 978/2012.
GSP graduation
The automatic suspension of GSP preferences for a specific product section (Art. 8) or an entire country (Art. 4) when imports exceed competitive thresholds. Indonesia's Section 3 graduated on 1 Jan 2020.
GSP+ (Special Incentive Arrangement)
An enhanced GSP arrangement providing duty-free access for vulnerable countries that ratify and implement 27+ international conventions on human rights, labour, environment, and good governance.
HS (Harmonized System)
The WCO's 6-digit international classification system used by 150+ countries. The first 6 digits are identical worldwide. The EU extends it to 8 digits (CN) and 10 digits (TARIC).
HSEN (Harmonized System Explanatory Notes)
The WCO's official commentary on HS classification. Provides authoritative interpretation of headings and subheadings.
Industrial use
The oil is destined for industrial processing (biodiesel, soap, lubricants, paints, etc.), not for human consumption, cosmetics, or pharmaceuticals. Determines the 40/60 vs. 51/59/91/99 split in heading 1515 90.
ISCC EU (International Sustainability and Carbon Certification)
The most widely used certification scheme for RED II compliance. Required if the biodiesel producer wants to count the fuel toward EU renewable energy targets. Also relevant for the ADD biofuel exemption.
Iodine value (IV)
A measure of the degree of unsaturation of an oil. Higher IV = more double bonds = more unsaturated. Used in the ADD product scope definition (IV < 105 g/100g for C6–C18 fatty acids).

M–R

MFN (Most-Favoured-Nation)
The standard WTO duty rate applied to all members equally. Same as ERGA OMNES. The baseline rate before any preferential reduction.
Neutralisation (alkali refining)
A refining step that removes free fatty acids using caustic soda (NaOH). The by-product is soapstock, which when acidulated becomes acid oil (heading 3823). Oils that have been neutralised are “refined.”
Nomenclature Committee (TARIC Committee)
The EU committee (Art. 7 of Reg. 2658/87) that reviews and approves CN nomenclature changes. Composed of Member State representatives, chaired by the Commission.
Origin
The country where the goods were wholly obtained or sufficiently processed. Determines eligibility for preferential rates (GSP, FTA). Proven by certificate of origin (Form A for GSP, EUR.1 for FTAs).
PFAD (Palm Fatty Acid Distillate)
The volatile fatty acids recovered during physical refining of palm oil. Classified under 3823 19 30 at 2.9%. Not subject to ADD because typical PFAD has DoS <97%.
Physical refining
A refining method that removes FFA by steam distillation under high vacuum (instead of alkali neutralisation). The by-product is fatty acid distillate (heading 3823 19 30), not acid oil.
Post-clearance audit
A customs review of import declarations after goods have been released. Can occur up to 3 years after import. May result in reclassification, back-duties, and penalties.
RED II (Renewable Energy Directive II)
Directive (EU) 2018/2001, establishing sustainability criteria for biofuels. Relevant because fatty acids certified under RED II are exempt from the ADD (Reg. 2023/111).
Refined oil
An oil that has undergone degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, deodorisation, or any processing beyond simple extraction and filtration. Refined oils are classified under different (higher-duty) CN subcodes than crude oils.

S–Z

Section (CCT)
A grouping of chapters in the Common Customs Tariff. Section 3 = Animal and vegetable fats and oils (Chapters 15). Section 6 = Chemical products (Chapters 28–38). GSP graduation operates by section.
Sensitive product (GSP)
A product category under GSP that receives only a partial duty reduction (3.5 percentage points) rather than full duty suspension. Most vegetable oils are “sensitive.”
Soapstock
The by-product of alkali refining: the hydrated soap formed when caustic soda reacts with FFA. When acidulated with sulphuric acid, becomes acid oil (heading 3823 19 90). When not acidulated, may be classified under 1522 00 91.
Suspension (autonomous tariff suspension)
A temporary reduction of duty to zero for specific goods intended for particular end uses (e.g., incorporation into ships/platforms). Reg. 3211/24 provides for suspensions on certain heading 3823 products.
TARIC (Integrated Tariff of the European Union)
The 10-digit EU working classification code. Extends the 8-digit CN with 2 additional digits for specific measures (ADD, CVD, quotas, suspensions). Updated daily by DG TAXUD.
Tariff escalation
The pattern of increasing duty rates from raw materials to processed goods. E.g., crude industrial oil (3.2%) < crude food oil (6.4%) < refined food oil (9.6%) < retail-pack oil (12.8%).
Technical/industrial use
See “Industrial use.” The CN distinguishes code 40/60 (technical) from 51/59/91/99 (other) based on this criterion.
Transesterification
The chemical process of converting triglyceride oils into fatty acid methyl esters (FAME/biodiesel) by reacting with methanol in the presence of a catalyst.
UCC (Union Customs Code)
Regulation (EU) 952/2013. The principal legal framework for EU customs procedures, including declarations, valuation, origin, and enforcement.
WCO (World Customs Organization)
The intergovernmental body that manages the Harmonized System (HS). Based in Brussels. HS revisions occur approximately every 5–7 years.
