Classification follows the General Interpretative Rules (GIR) in order: GIR 1 — heading text and notes decide first; GIR 3(a) — most specific description wins; GIR 3(b) — classify by essential character; GIR 3(c) — last numerical heading.

It's called tariff escalation: raw materials face lower duties to support EU processors; finished goods face higher duties to protect EU manufacturers. The more processing, the higher the rate. Crude industrial oil (3.2%) → crude food oil (6.4%) → refined oil (9.6%).

MFN and ERGA OMNES are identical — the standard WTO duty rate. GSP is a reduced rate for developing countries. GSP+ and EBA offer deeper preferences. FTA rates (e.g., future EU-Indonesia CEPA) are bilateral preferential rates, often 0% after phase-in.

From 1 January 2027, all Indonesian products pay full MFN rates. For RSO, the impact is zero — Section 3 was already graduated in 2020. The real change is the EU-Indonesia CEPA, which will progressively reduce MFN rates to zero once in force.
