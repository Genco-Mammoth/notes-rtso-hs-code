Full Legal Description

CN 1515 90 40 00

“Other fixed vegetable fats and oils and their fractions, whether or not refined, but not chemically modified — Other oils and their fractions — Crude oils — For technical or industrial uses”

This code covers crude (unrefined) fixed vegetable or microbial oils that are not intended for human consumption, cosmetic, or pharmaceutical use. The oil must be both:

  1. Crude — extracted by mechanical pressing or solvent extraction, without further refining (no degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, or deodorisation)
  2. For technical or industrial use — destined for industrial processing (e.g., biodiesel production, soap making, lubricant manufacture, paint production)

Reg. (EU) 2024/1652

Typical products classified here

Industrial feedstocks
  • Crude rubber seed oil (biodiesel feedstock)
  • Crude neem oil (biopesticide production)
  • Crude jatropha oil (biofuel feedstock)
  • Crude castor oil (industrial grade, if not under 1515 30)
  • Crude kapok seed oil (industrial use)
  • Crude cottonseed oil (technical grade, if not under 1512)
Technical applications
  • Biodiesel / HVO feedstock (transesterification)
  • Soap and detergent manufacturing
  • Lubricant and grease production
  • Paint, varnish, and coating formulations
  • Leather treatment / fat liquoring
  • Epoxy resin plasticisers

Why this rate is low

Two factors combine: (1) crude state means the oil is an unprocessed raw material, and (2) industrial end use means it feeds EU manufacturing rather than competing with EU consumer products. The EU tariff structure consistently favours raw material imports for European industry.

Savings vs. default

Compared to the default non-technical crude code (1515 90 59 at 6.4%), this code saves 3.2 percentage points — a 50% duty reduction. For a 1,000-tonne shipment at €800/t CIF, that is €25,600 in duty savings.

Classification Criteria: Two-Part Test

To legitimately classify under 1515 90 40, the importer must satisfy customs on two criteria:

1
Is the oil crude?
The oil must be in its natural extracted state. No chemical refining, degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, or deodorisation. Mechanical filtering to remove solid impurities (seed meal, sediment) is acceptable and does not change crude status. High FFA content (25–35%) is acceptable for crude oils.
↓
2
Is it for technical or industrial use?
The oil must be destined for industrial processing, not for direct human consumption, cosmetic, or pharmaceutical applications. Biodiesel feedstock qualifies — the oil undergoes transesterification or hydrotreatment in an industrial fuel production facility. Evidence: end-use declaration, supply agreement with EU industrial buyer, purchase contract referencing industrial use.
What makes it “technical/industrial”?
The CN does not define “technical or industrial use” in the abstract. Customs authorities look at objective evidence: the stated end use on import documents, the nature of the buyer (e.g., a biodiesel plant vs. a food distributor), the product specifications (high FFA, unrefined), and whether the oil is fit for human consumption without further processing. The combination of crude state + industrial buyer + high FFA makes a compelling case.

Relevance to Rubber Seed Oil

This code is the recommended classification for crude rubber seed oil imported from Indonesia as biodiesel feedstock. See the Rubber Seed Oil Analysis for the full strategic classification analysis.

Why 1515 90 40 fits rubber seed oil
  • Crude: RSO is mechanically expelled or solvent extracted without refining — confirmed by high FFA (25–35%)
  • Industrial use: Biodiesel feedstock is an industrial end use — the oil is transesterified into FAME or hydrotreated into HVO
  • Not food: RSO at 25–35% FFA is not fit for human consumption without further refining
  • No trade defence: No anti-dumping or countervailing duties apply to heading 1515 codes
