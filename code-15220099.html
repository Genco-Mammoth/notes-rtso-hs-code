MFN: 0% No ADD / CVD Chapter 15 — Section 3 GSP graduated (Section 3)

The residual catch-all for materials left over from the industrial treatment of fats and oils that are not classified elsewhere under heading 1522. At 0% MFN with no trade defence measures, it is the lowest-duty code in the entire Chapter 15/38 landscape — but rubber seed oil is a primary product, not a residue, and cannot be classified here.

1. Full Legal Description

CN 1522 00 99 — Heading 1522 (Chapter 15)

“Degras; residues resulting from the treatment of fatty substances or animal or vegetable waxes — Other — Other”

This code covers leftover materials from the industrial processing of fats, oils, and waxes that do not fit into more specific subheadings. Key qualifying criterion: the product must be a residue (a secondary output from treating a primary fatty substance), not a primary product itself.

Examples of products that genuinely belong here:

  • Spent bleaching earth containing residual oil (after oil refining)
  • Residual filter cakes from fatty acid production
  • Lecithin processing residues not classified elsewhere
  • Miscellaneous fatty residues from industrial processing

3. Trade Defence: Clean

No ADD, no CVD, no surveillance

Heading 1522 00 has no anti-dumping duties and no countervailing duties applicable to any origin, including Indonesia. It is one of the few headings in the fatty products landscape completely free of trade defence measures. This makes it theoretically the cheapest classification — if the product genuinely qualifies as a residue.

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Genuine residues
  • Spent bleaching earth with residual oil
  • Filter press residues from fatty acid processing
  • Lecithin extraction residues
  • Wax recovery residues from oil dewaxing
  • Miscellaneous fatty substance treatment residues
Must NOT be
  • Primary extracted oils — heading 1515
  • Soapstocks — 1522 00 91 (3.2%)
  • Degras — 1522 00 10 (3.8%)
  • Acid oils from refining — heading 3823
  • Olive oil residues — 1522 00 31/39

8. Relevance to Rubber Seed Oil

Assessment: NOT defensible
RSO is a primary product, not a residue
CriterionResidue (1522 00 99)Rubber seed oil
Product natureLeftover from treating a fatty substancePrimary output of seed pressing
Origin processSecondary output (filter cake, spent earth, etc.)Main product (pressing/extraction)
Value relationshipLow-value by-productFull-value primary oil
EU classificationChapter 15 heading 1522 (residues)Chapter 15 heading 1515 (fixed oils)

Bottom line: Rubber seed oil is the main intended output of rubber seed processing, not a leftover from another operation. Attempting to classify RSO under 1522 00 99 to access the 0% rate would be a clear misdeclaration. Customs authorities routinely reject this type of classification for primary vegetable oils. The correct classification is 1515 90 40 at 3.2%.
