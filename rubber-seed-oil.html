Definitive Answer
The correct HS code for crude rubber seed oil from Indonesia as biodiesel feedstock is CN 1515 90 40 — at 3.2% duty

This is the only code that is simultaneously (a) legally defensible, (b) delivers the lowest possible duty rate without trade-defence exposure, and (c) can be supported with objective evidence. All cheaper-looking alternatives — 0% codes under Chapters 15 and 38 — are either indefensible misclassifications or carry prohibitive anti-dumping/countervailing duties.

Rubber seed oil is not individually named in the Harmonized System or the EU Combined Nomenclature. It falls under the “other” fixed vegetable oils — heading 1515 90 by default. The strategic question is whether alternative headings (Chapter 38, Chapter 15 residues, or Chapter 15 chemically modified) can be legitimately and defensibly applied given the product's high FFA and industrial end use.

2. Classification Decision Path

The classification of rubber seed oil depends on two critical questions. Each path leads to different headings, duty rates, and trade-defence risks:

A
Is the oil a by-product of alkali refining?
“Acid oils from refining” (heading 3823) are specifically the acidulated by-product of chemical (alkali) refining of crude oils — soapstock that has been acidulated. Crude rubber seed oil is NOT a refining by-product. It is a primary product obtained by pressing/extraction of rubber seeds. It naturally has high FFA, but it is not “acid oil from refining.”
↓
B
Is the oil a residue from fatty substance treatment?
Heading 1522 covers degras, soapstocks, oil foots, and other residues from the treatment of fatty substances. Rubber seed oil is not a residue — it is the primary extracted product, not a leftover from another process.
↓
C
Has the oil been chemically modified?
Heading 1518 covers oils that have been boiled, oxidised, dehydrated, sulphurised, blown, polymerised, or otherwise chemically modified. Crude rubber seed oil is not chemically modified — it is simply extracted and possibly filtered. This path would only apply if the oil undergoes chemical processing before import.
↓
D
Default: heading 1515 90 — “Other fixed vegetable oils”
Rubber seed oil is a fixed vegetable oil not specifically named elsewhere in Chapter 15. It falls under 1515 90. The sub-heading within 1515 90 depends on: (1) crude vs. refined, and (2) end use (technical/industrial vs. other).

4. Recommended Path: CN 1515 90 40 at 3.2%

Optimal classification
CN 1515 90 40 — Crude fixed vegetable oils for technical or industrial uses

Rubber seed oil imported as biodiesel feedstock can be classified under 1515 90 40 (crude oils, for technical or industrial uses) at an MFN duty rate of 3.2%. This is the lowest rate achievable through a defensible classification that does not trigger anti-dumping or countervailing duties.

Why this classification is defensible

Criterion 1: Crude state

Rubber seed oil that has only been mechanically expelled or solvent extracted, without further refining, degumming, or neutralisation, is crude under CN rules. Codes 1515 90 40 and 1515 90 59 both cover crude oils; the distinction between them is end use, not processing state.

Criterion 2: Technical / industrial use

Biodiesel feedstock is an industrial use. The oil is not destined for human consumption, cosmetics, or pharmaceuticals — it will undergo transesterification or hydrotreatment in an industrial fuel production facility. The CN distinguishes codes 40 (technical/industrial) from 59 (other) precisely on this basis.

Documentation required to support classification

Evidence pack for customs
  • End-use declaration: A statement that the goods are imported for use as biodiesel feedstock (industrial use, not for human consumption)
  • Purchase contract / proforma invoice referencing the industrial end use and CN code 1515 90 40
  • Technical specification sheet showing: FFA content (25–35%), acid value, iodine value, moisture, unsaponifiable matter
  • Lab analysis / certificate of analysis from an accredited laboratory confirming the product is crude (not refined) and unsuitable for food use without further processing
  • Letter of intent / supply agreement with the EU biodiesel producer confirming the oil will be used exclusively as feedstock
BTI strongly recommended
Before committing to large-volume shipments, apply for a Binding Tariff Information (BTI) decision from the customs authority of the importing Member State. A BTI provides legal certainty on the classification for up to 3 years and is binding across all EU Member States. This is especially important because the distinction between codes 40 (industrial) and 59 (other) depends on documented end use, which customs may scrutinise.

6. Why Not Heading 3823 (Acid Oils)?

The MFN rate of 2.9% under 3823 19 90 or 3823 19 30 looks attractive on paper — lower than 1515 90 40's 3.2%. But this path is not recommended for rubber seed oil, for three independent reasons:

Reason 1
Misclassification risk

Heading 3823 covers “acid oils from refining” — the acidulated by-product of alkali refining of crude oils (soapstock that has been treated with acid). Rubber seed oil is a primary product from seed pressing/extraction, not a refining by-product. Classifying it here would be a misclassification that customs can challenge.

Reason 2
ADD exposure (49.3%)

If the RSO has a DoS ≥97% and meets the C6–C18 / iodine value criteria, CN 3823 19 90 triggers the anti-dumping duty of 46.4% (residual rate) on top of the 2.9% MFN, resulting in a 49.3% effective duty. This is far worse than any heading 1515 option.

Reason 3
Post-clearance audit risk

Even if initial declarations pass customs, the EU can conduct post-clearance audits for up to 3 years. If reclassified retroactively, the importer faces back-duties, penalties, and interest. The financial exposure of a wrong 3823 classification far outweighs the 0.3 pp savings vs. 1515 90 40.

⚠ The “High FFA” Trap

Rubber seed oil with 25–35% FFA is chemically similar to acid oils from refining, which can also have FFA of 30–70%. This chemical similarity may tempt a classification under heading 3823. However, EU classification rules are based on the origin and processing of the product, not solely on its chemical composition.

GIR (General Interpretative Rules) 1 and 6 require classification by the heading that most specifically describes the product. Rubber seed oil is a fixed vegetable oil extracted from seeds — that is heading 1515 by definition. It is not an industrial chemical by-product (heading 3823). Customs will look at how the product was made, not just its FFA percentage.

10. What This Means For Your Business

Use this code
1515 90 40 — 3.2% duty

Declare crude RSO as “crude fixed vegetable oil for technical/industrial use.” Your invoice should reference 1515 90 40 and state “for industrial use as biodiesel feedstock.” Get a BTI before your first shipment.

Avoid these codes
3823 19 90 and 3823 19 10

These carry a 46.4% anti-dumping duty for Indonesia. Even if GSP reduces the MFN to 0%, the ADD of 46.4% still applies. The total effective rate is 49.3%. Do not use Chapter 38 codes for RSO.

Consider eventually
Blending to access 0% via 1518 00 31

If you blend RSO with at least 20% of another inedible oil (e.g., jatropha), the mixture may qualify for 0% duty. This requires a BTI, genuine physical blending, and documentation. Not worth it for small volumes; becomes economical above ~500 tonnes/year.

Watch this
EU-Indonesia CEPA

Once the EU-Indonesia free trade agreement enters into force (concluded September 2025, not yet ratified), duties on most goods will be progressively eliminated. At that point, the optimal classification may shift from “which code has the lowest rate” to “which code the CEPA covers first.” Until then, use 1515 90 40.

Three-Sentence Summary

1. Declare crude RSO under 1515 90 40 at 3.2% duty — this is the correct classification and the lowest defensible rate as of July 2026. 2. Obtain a BTI ruling and prepare an evidence pack before importing. 3. If you can source at least 20% of a second inedible oil, explore the blending strategy for 0% duty under 1518 00 31, but only after legal review.
