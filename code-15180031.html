CN 1518 00 31 — Mixed Inedible Vegetable Oils, Crude, for Industrial Use

MFN: 0% CVD risk for biodiesel Chapter 15 — Section 3 GSP graduated (Section 3)

Mixtures of two or more fixed vegetable oils that are fluid, inedible, and intended for technical or industrial use. The crude variant carries 0% MFN. The critical requirement: it must be a genuine mixture — single-origin oils do not qualify.

1. Full Legal Description

CN 1518 00 31 — Heading 1518 (Chapter 15)

“Fixed vegetable oils, fluid, mixed, inedible, n.e.s., for technical or industrial uses other than the manufacture of foodstuffs for human consumption — Crude”

Four cumulative requirements must all be met:

#RequirementDetail
1MixedMust be a mixture of two or more different fixed vegetable oils. Single-origin oils are excluded.
2FluidLiquid at room temperature. Solid fats (e.g., shea butter) do not qualify.
3InedibleNot fit for human consumption, or not intended for food use. Must be documented.
4Industrial useFor technical or industrial purposes, excluding foodstuff production.
Single oils excluded
A pure, single-type vegetable oil (e.g., 100% rubber seed oil) cannot be classified under 1518 00 31 regardless of its end use. It must go under its specific heading (1515 90 for unmodified “other” vegetable oils). Only genuine blends of two or more oils qualify.

3. CVD Risk (Reg. EU 2019/2092)

If the mixed oil is used as biodiesel, the countervailing duty under Reg. 2019/2092 may apply. The CVD scope includes ex 1518 00 91, ex 1518 00 95, and ex 1518 00 99 — but not explicitly 1518 00 31. However, customs may argue that a mixed oil used as biodiesel falls within the spirit of the CVD scope. The legal position is ambiguous and should be confirmed via BTI.

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Genuine oil mixtures
  • RSO + jatropha oil blend (biodiesel feedstock)
  • Neem + karanja oil blend (biopesticide)
  • Mixed technical-grade seed oils (lubricant base)
  • Multi-feedstock crude oil blend (industrial processing)
  • Waste-vegetable-oil blend with crude (industrial fuel)
Must NOT be
  • Single-origin oil — goes to heading 1515
  • Edible oil blend — goes to heading 1517 or 1518 00 99
  • Solid fat blend — does not meet “fluid” requirement
  • Refined food oil mix — goes to Chapter 15 headings

Bottom line: If the Indonesian exporter blends RSO with another inedible oil (e.g., 50% RSO + 50% jatropha oil) specifically for industrial use, the mixture could qualify for 1518 00 31 at 0% MFN — saving the full 3.2% vs. classifying as pure RSO under 1515 90 40. However, the blend must be genuine (not just on paper), and the CVD ambiguity creates uncertainty if used as biodiesel feedstock. This strategy requires careful legal assessment.
