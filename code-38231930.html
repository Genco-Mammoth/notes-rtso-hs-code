CN 3823 19 30 — Fatty Acid Distillate

MFN: 2.9% No ADD (outside scope) Chapter 38 — Section 6 GSP non-sensitive

Fatty acid distillate — the volatile fatty acids recovered during the physical refining (steam deodorisation) of crude oils. The most commercially significant product is PFAD (palm fatty acid distillate). Crucially, this code is explicitly excluded from the anti-dumping duty on Indonesian fatty acids because typical distillates have a degree of split below the 97% threshold.

1. Full Legal Description

CN 3823 19 30 — Heading 3823 (Chapter 38)

“Industrial monocarboxylic fatty acids; acid oils from refining — Fatty acid distillate”

What this product actually is: The condensate recovered when crude oils undergo physical refining — a process where steam is injected under high vacuum at 200–260°C to strip free fatty acids and other volatile compounds from the oil. The stripped vapours are condensed and collected as fatty acid distillate. Unlike acid oils from alkali refining (3823 19 90), these distillates are not treated with chemicals — they are a physical separation product.

Key product characteristics:

  • FFA: typically 70–95%
  • Degree of split (DoS): typically 81–95% — below the 97% ADD threshold
  • Unsaponifiable matter: 2–10%
  • Colour: variable (yellow-brown to dark)

Best-case scenario for Indonesian fatty products
For Indonesian-origin PFAD or other fatty acid distillates, the GSP non-sensitive status means the effective duty is 0% — the lowest possible rate for any fatty product from Indonesia. However, Indonesia graduates entirely from GSP on 1 January 2027, after which the rate reverts to 2.9% MFN.

3. Why No Anti-Dumping Duty Applies

Explicit exclusion from Reg. 2023/111
DoS threshold mechanism

The EU anti-dumping regulation on Indonesian fatty acids (Reg. 2023/111) defines its product scope using a degree of split (DoS) threshold of ≥ 97%. Fatty acid distillates like PFAD typically have a DoS between 81% and 95% — below the 97% threshold — meaning they fall outside the ADD scope.

The Commission explicitly addressed this in Recitals 80–90 of the regulation, recognising that fatty acid distillates are by-products of biodiesel/food oil production and should not be caught by the anti-dumping measure.

ADD product scopeFatty acids C6–C18, IV <105, DoS ≥97%
Typical PFAD DoS81–95%
ResultOutside scope — no ADD
Caveat: DoS must be verified
If a particular fatty acid distillate has been further processed and achieves DoS ≥97%, it would enter the ADD scope and be reclassified to 3823 19 10 (distilled) or 3823 19 90 (other). Always verify the DoS on the certificate of analysis before import.

4. Typical Products Classified Here

Physical refining distillates
  • PFAD (palm fatty acid distillate) — the dominant product
  • Soybean fatty acid distillate (SAD)
  • Sunflower fatty acid distillate
  • Rapeseed/canola fatty acid distillate
  • Coconut fatty acid distillate
  • PKFAD (palm kernel fatty acid distillate)
  • Tallow fatty acid distillate (animal origin)
Industrial applications
  • Biodiesel feedstock (esterification to FAME)
  • HVO (hydrotreated vegetable oil) feedstock
  • Animal feed (under TARIC ...80, ...30)
  • Oleochemical feedstock (soap, candles)
  • Fatty acid distillation feedstock
  • Industrial boiler fuel (low-value use)

Bottom line: Rubber seed oil has not undergone steam deodorisation or physical refining. It is a crude vegetable oil, not a distillate. The correct classification for RSO as industrial feedstock is 1515 90 40 at 3.2%.
