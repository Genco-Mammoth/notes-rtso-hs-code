POME — Palm Oil Mill Effluent Oil

Biodiesel feedstock From Indonesia Classification disputed

Oil recovered from palm oil mill effluent — the wastewater sludge from crude palm oil milling. A contentious biodiesel feedstock under active EU fraud investigation. Classification sits at a disputed boundary between Chapter 15 residues (1522 00 99, 0%) and Chapter 38 acid oils (3823 19 90, 2.9% + ADD risk), with no settled EU-wide position.

1. Product Profile

Product Definition
Palm Oil Mill Effluent Oil (POME Oil)
Source
Wastewater sludge from crude palm oil (CPO) milling. Oil is recovered from the effluent ponds via skimming or dissolved air flotation.
Composition
Recovered oil/grease with high FFA (typically 50–80%), mixed with water, sediment, and biological matter.
Volume
Major export from Indonesia and Malaysia. EU imports surged in 2023–2025 as POME oil was used for biodiesel under RED II double-counting.
EU scrutiny
Accused of being "palm oil in disguise" by environmental groups. Fraud investigations ongoing. Some Member States restrict POME-based biodiesel.

⚠ Classification uncertainty

POME oil sits at a classification boundary. If the oil is simply recovered/skimmed from effluent ponds, 1522 00 99 (residue, 0%) may apply. If it has been acidulated or chemically treated, 3823 19 90 (acid oil, 2.9%) may apply. EU customs authorities are increasingly scrutinising POME imports due to fraud concerns.

Bottom line for Indonesian exporters
POME oil can be cheaper at the border than RSO (0% if accepted as residue vs 3.2% for RSO), but carries substantial regulatory, classification, and reputational risk. Multiple EU Member States are restricting or investigating POME-based biodiesel. RSO offers a more stable, defensible position for new market entrants who want to avoid the palm oil controversy entirely.
