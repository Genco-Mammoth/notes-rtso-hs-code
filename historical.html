Historical Duty Rate Evolution

Primary source: Reg. (EU) 2024/1652 Jan 2025 · Jan 2026 · Jul 2026

Tracking how duty rates for HS 1515.90 codes have changed over time, with the legal instruments behind each change. The most significant recent event was the entry into force of Regulation (EU) 2024/1652 on 1 July 2024.

Rate Evolution Timeline

Before 1 July 2024
Pre-Regulation 2024/1652 period
The duty rate landscape for heading 1515 90 was governed by the standard CCT rates in Annex I to Regulation (EEC) 2658/87. See the discrepancy box below regarding the exact pre-existing rates.
24 June 2024 — Regulation adopted
Council Regulation (EU) 2024/1652 published
The Council adopted a regulation amending Annex I to Reg. 2658/87, setting out the duty rates for cereals, oilseeds and derived products (including heading 1515 90) with footnote (6) imposing a 50% surcharge on Russia/Belarus-origin goods.

Reg. (EU) 2024/1652
1 July 2024 — Regulation applied
New duty rates take effect
The rates stated in Reg. 2024/1652 became the legally applicable rates: 1515 90 59 at 6.4%, 1515 90 99 at 9.6%, and 50% for RU/BY origins across all six codes.
January 2025 — No change
Rates unchanged from July 2024
The CN 2025 Implementing Regulation (Reg. (EU) 2024/2020) maintained the same duty rates. No modifications to heading 1515 90 rates.
January 2026 — No change
Rates unchanged from 2025
The CN 2026 Implementing Regulation (Reg. (EU) 2025/1926) maintained the same duty rates. Rates for 1515 90 59 remain at 6.4% and 1515 90 99 at 9.6%.
4 July 2026 — Current
Reference date for this report
Rates confirmed as 6.4% (1515 90 59) and 9.6% (1515 90 99) for MFN origins; 50% for Russia/Belarus. Indonesia pays MFN rate (no preferences).

Source Discrepancy: Pre-July 2024 Rates

⚠ Conflicting data on pre-Regulation 2024/1652 rates

The rates applicable to CN 1515 90 59 and 1515 90 99 before 1 July 2024 are reported differently across sources. This is the most significant data discrepancy encountered in this report.

Position A: Rates were 0% (duty-free) before Reg. 2024/1652

tariffnumber.com (2024) Analysis of the regulation recitals suggests rates were zero before the regulation. Recital (3) states tariffs for these products “are either set at zero or very low.” Recital (6) mentions measures needed when duties “are set at zero or are not sufficiently high.”
celna24.pl Currently shows ERGA OMNES (excl. RU/BY) as 0% for these codes, with only RU/BY at 50%. This interpretation would mean the rates in Reg. 2024/1652 are only the RU/BY surcharges.

Position B: Rates of 6.4% / 9.6% pre-existed and were restated

EUR-Lex text The regulation text restates the rates alongside footnote (6). One reading is that the regulation simply needed to cite the existing rates to attach the RU/BY exception footnote. The EU tariff structure has long distinguished crude vs. refined oils at different rates.
Assessment: The EUR-Lex text of Reg. 2024/1652 is the primary source. It states the rates as 6,4 [(6)] and 9,6 [(6)]. Regardless of what existed before, these are the current legally applicable MFN rates for all origins except RU/BY.

The pre-July 2024 rates cannot be definitively resolved from the regulation text alone — one would need to consult the CCT Annex I as it stood on 30 June 2024 (via the EUR-Lex consolidated version of Reg. 2658/87). For practical purposes, the current rates are what matters: Indonesia pays 6.4% and 9.6% as of 4 July 2026.
