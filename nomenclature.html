Nomenclature System: HS → CN → TARIC

Primary sources only WCO HS 2022 Reg. 2658/87

EU customs classification is a layered system that adds specificity at each level. Understanding how the 6-digit international code expands into 8-digit EU codes and 10-digit TARIC subcodes is essential for determining the correct duty rate.

The Three-Layer Classification System

HS Code (6 digits) — WCO
International standard. Used by 150+ countries.
Digits 1–2: Chapter | 3–4: Heading | 5–6: Subheading
↓ EU adds digits 7–8
CN Code (8 digits) — EU
Combined Nomenclature. Determines the base duty rate.
Digits 7–8: EU-specific subdivision. Published annually.
↓ EU adds digits 9–10
TARIC Code (10 digits) — EU
Further subdivision for specific measures (anti-dumping, quotas, suspensions).
Updated daily by DG TAXUD.
When digits are “00”
If a CN code has no EU-specific subdivision at digits 7–8, they are 00. Similarly, if a TARIC code has no further subdivision at digits 9–10, they are 00. For example, 1515 90 59 00 is a valid full 10-digit TARIC code.

Heading 1515: Full Structure

Heading 1515 covers “Other fixed vegetable or microbial fats and oils (including jojoba oil) and their fractions, whether or not refined, but not chemically modified.” The word “Other” means these are fats and oils not specifically named in earlier headings (soya-bean oil, olive oil, palm oil, sunflower oil, etc. are all classified elsewhere in Chapter 15).

HS subheading Description Focus of this report
1515 11 / 19 Linseed oil and its fractions
1515 21 / 29 Maize (corn) oil and its fractions
1515 30 Castor oil and its fractions
1515 50 Sesame oil and its fractions
1515 90 Other (all remaining fixed vegetable/microbial fats and oils) ← Yes
What falls under 1515 90?
Heading 1515 90 (“Other”) captures fats and oils that do not have their own dedicated subheading. These include: jojoba oil, tung oil, oiticica oil, hazelnut oil, avocado oil, hemp seed oil, rice bran oil, walnut oil, shea butter, mango kernel oil, tamanu oil, microbial oils, and many others. Essentially any fixed vegetable or microbial fat/oil not specifically named elsewhere.

Subheading 1515 90: CN 8-digit Breakdown

The EU further subdivides HS subheading 1515 90 into CN 8-digit codes. The subdivision follows two key distinctions:

Distinction 1: Crude vs. Refined

Crude oils (unrefined) are classified in CN codes ending in 40, 51, and 59.

Refined or non-crude oils (including those that have been processed beyond crude state) are classified in codes ending in 60, 91, and 99.

Distinction 2: End use

Codes ending in 40 and 60 cover oils for technical or industrial uses (non-food).

The remaining codes (51, 59, 91, 99) cover oils for other uses (typically food, cosmetics, pharmaceutical).

How to Classify a Product Under 1515 90

The following decision tree shows how to determine the correct CN 8-digit code for a given vegetable oil or fat. For a comprehensive guide with worked examples and common pitfalls, see the Classification Guide.

Step 1: Is it a “fixed” vegetable or microbial fat/oil, not chemically modified?
If NO → does not fall under heading 1515. If YES → continue.
↓
Step 2: Is it specifically named in another subheading? (linseed, maize, castor, sesame)
If YES → classify under 1515 11/19, 21/29, 30, or 50. If NO → it falls under 1515 90.
↓
Step 3: Is it crude (unrefined) or refined/non-crude?
Crude → codes 40, 51, 59. Refined → codes 60, 91, 99.
↓
Step 4: Is it for technical/industrial use?
YES → code 40 (crude) or 60 (refined). NO → continue.
↓
Step 5: Is it solid in packings ≤ 1 kg?
YES → code 51 (crude) or 91 (refined). NO (solid > 1 kg or fluid/liquid) → code 59 (crude) or 99 (refined).

TARIC 10-digit Subcodes

While CN codes determine the base duty rate, TARIC 10-digit codes may be needed to apply specific measures (e.g., anti-dumping duties, tariff suspensions). For the two codes in focus:

CN Code (8-digit) TARIC Subcode (10-digit) Additional specificity
1515 90 59 1515 90 59 00 No further TARIC subdivision — the “catch-all” code
1515 90 99 1515 90 99 00 Base code (general)
1515 90 99 20 Hazelnut oil
1515 90 99 92 Refined oil containing ≥35% arachidonic acid or ≥35% docosahexaenoic acid
1515 90 99 99 Other (not elsewhere specified)
For practical purposes
All TARIC subcodes under CN 1515 90 59 and 1515 90 99 carry the same duty rates. The 10-digit subdivision matters only for specific measures (if any exist for a particular product type) and for statistical purposes.
