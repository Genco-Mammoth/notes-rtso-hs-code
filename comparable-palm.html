Palm Oil Products — The Dominant Feedstock Family

5 products analysed Headings 1511 & 1513 Section 3 (graduated — no GSP) EUDR applies

Indonesia is the world's largest producer of palm oil. These five products — CPO, CPKO, RBD PO, RBD Palm Olein, and RPKO — dominate the global vegetable oil trade and are the reason Indonesia's Section 3 was graduated from EU GSP preferences. Every alternative feedstock, including rubber seed oil, competes against this family on price, availability, and sustainability perception.

1. Market Context: Why Palm Dominates

Indonesia's position
World's #1 palm oil producer

Indonesia produces approximately 46 million tonnes of palm oil annually — over 55% of global supply. Palm oil and its derivatives are Indonesia's largest agricultural export. The sheer scale means palm products set the baseline price for all vegetable oil feedstocks in the Asian market.

EU regulatory headwinds
EUDR, RED II, and sustainability

Palm oil faces increasing regulatory pressure in the EU: EUDR (Reg. 2023/1115) requires deforestation-free certification. RED II phases out palm-based biodiesel (high ILUC risk) from 2030. Section 3 graduation means no GSP preferences — full MFN rates apply. These pressures create space for alternative feedstocks like RSO.

2. Heading 1511 — Palm Oil and Its Fractions

Product Descriptions

CPO
Crude Palm Oil

1511 10 90 — Unrefined oil pressed from oil palm fruit mesocarp. Deep orange-red colour from carotenoids. High in saturated fat (~50%). The base product from which all refined palm products are derived. Used directly as biodiesel feedstock (transesterification to PME) or refined for food.

RBD PO
Refined, Bleached, Deodorised Palm Oil

1511 90 99 — CPO that has undergone full refining: degumming, neutralisation, bleaching, deodorisation. Neutral flavour, pale colour. Used primarily for food but also as biodiesel feedstock where refined quality is specified.

RBD Palm Olein
RBD Palm Olein (Liquid Fraction)

1511 90 91 — The liquid fraction obtained by fractionating RBD palm oil after crystallisation. More unsaturated than whole palm oil. Used primarily as cooking oil but increasingly as biodiesel feedstock in tropical countries.

Key insight: crude is duty-free, refined is expensive
CPO imported as biodiesel feedstock can enter at 0% under 1511 10 10 (technical/industrial use) — the same end-use logic as RSO under 1515 90 40. But refined palm products face 9.6% — higher even than RSO at 3.2%. This creates an incentive to import crude palm oil for EU processing rather than refined products.

3. Heading 1513 — Palm Kernel and Babassu Oil

Product Descriptions

CPKO
Crude Palm Kernel Oil

1513 21 90 — Oil pressed from the kernel (seed) of the oil palm fruit, not the mesocarp. Chemically distinct from palm oil: high in lauric acid (C12, ~48%), similar to coconut oil. Used primarily in oleochemicals (soaps, detergents, surfactants), less commonly as biodiesel feedstock due to higher value in chemical applications.

RPKO
Refined Palm Kernel Oil

1513 29 90 — CPKO after refining. Same lauric profile. Used in food (confectionery fats, cocoa butter substitutes) and cosmetics. Rarely used as biodiesel feedstock due to high food/oleochemical value.

Palm advantage
Massive scale, crude at 0%

CPO imported as industrial feedstock enters at 0% duty — beating RSO's 3.2%. Indonesia produces 46 million tonnes annually. The infrastructure, logistics, and buyer relationships are all established. For a biodiesel plant that can accept palm-based feedstock, CPO is the economic default.

RSO advantage
Sustainability story, EUDR exemption

Rubber seed oil is a non-food, waste-recovery crop. Unlike palm oil, it is unlikely to trigger EUDR requirements (no deforestation linkage). It carries no palm-associated reputational risk. For EU buyers facing sustainability mandates or palm phase-out commitments, RSO offers a credible alternative at a modest 3.2% duty premium over CPO.

Strategic positioning for RSO
Rubber seed oil cannot compete with CPO on price (0% vs 3.2%) or volume (niche crop vs 46M tonnes). RSO's value proposition is differentiation: a non-palm, non-food, waste-recovery feedstock for EU buyers who need to demonstrate palm-free supply chains. Price RSO against RBD PO (9.6%) for food-grade buyers, and against PFAD (2.9%) for biodiesel buyers. At 3.2%, RSO sits between the two and offers a unique sustainability narrative neither can match.
