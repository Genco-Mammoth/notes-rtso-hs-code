UCO — Used Cooking Oil

CN 1518 00 99 MFN: 0% RED II double-counting

Waste cooking oil collected from commercial and household frying. The largest biodiesel feedstock imported into the EU by volume. Classified under 1518 00 99 at 0% MFN under Reg. (EU) 2024/1652, but subject to strict import controls under Reg. (EU) 2025/2181 (October 2025). Eligible for double-counting under RED II Annex IX Part B, making it the most economically attractive feedstock — when genuine.

1. Product Profile

CN 1518 00 99 — Heading 1518 (Chapter 15)

"Inedible mixtures or preparations of animal, vegetable or microbial fats or oils or of fractions of different fats or oils of this chapter, not elsewhere specified or included — Other"

Source
Post-consumer waste oil from commercial and household frying. Collected, filtered, and sometimes pre-treated before export.
Composition
Mixed triglycerides with elevated FFA (2–15%) from thermal degradation. May contain food residues, water, and polymers.
EU regulatory status
Annex IX Part B of RED II — counts double toward renewable energy targets. Import conditions tightened by Reg. (EU) 2025/2181 (Oct 2025).
Fraud concerns
Significant risk of virgin palm oil being misdeclared as UCO to access RED II double-counting benefits. EU customs and sustainability auditors actively investigating.

Bottom line for Indonesian exporters
UCO is the benchmark feedstock: 0% duty, RED II double-counting, and high demand. But the market is fiercely competitive, fraud-prone, and increasingly regulated. RSO is a viable alternative for buyers who cannot secure reliable UCO volumes — accepting 3.2% duty in exchange for supply certainty, simpler classification, and lower fraud risk.
