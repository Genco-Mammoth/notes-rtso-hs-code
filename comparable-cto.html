1. Product Profile

CN 3803 00 10 — Heading 3803 (Chapter 38)

"Tall oil, whether or not refined — Crude"

Source
Acidulation of the soap skimmings (black liquor soap) from kraft pulping of softwoods (primarily pine). A by-product of the paper industry.
Composition
35–50% fatty acids (mostly C18 unsaturated), 35–50% rosin acids, 5–15% unsaponifiables. Dark, viscous liquid with distinctive odour.
Use as biodiesel
CTO is hydrotreated to produce HVO (hydrotreated vegetable oil) — chemically identical to fossil diesel. Major HVO producers (Neste, Preem, UPM) use CTO as feedstock.
Availability
Limited supply — tied to kraft pulp production. Global production ~2 million tonnes/year. Scandinavia and US Southeast are main sources. Indonesia is NOT a significant CTO exporter.

CTO can be further refined into tall oil fatty acids (3823 13 00, 2.9%) or distilled tall oil (3803 00 90, ~4.1%). The crude variant is the most common import form for HVO production.

Bottom line
CTO is a premium feedstock for HVO producers but Indonesia is not a significant source — CTO is a Scandinavian/North American product. For an Indonesian exporter, CTO is not a viable alternative. RSO is the logical choice from an Indonesian supply base.
